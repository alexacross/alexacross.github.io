Halloween Webpage

What is Halloween?

Halloween is a holiday celebrated in a multitude of countries on the last day of October every year and was considered as the dedicated time to remember the deceased. Its many traditions have originated from ancient Celtic Harvest Festivals and dates back to about 1745. Today, many forms of celebration include trick-or- treating, costume parties, jack-o-latern making, bonfires, and paying visits to haunted houses and other attractions.

Trick-or-Treating

The concept of trick-or-treating has been a tradition in North America since the 1920s, although it goes as far back as the 16th century in Britain and Ireland. During this activity, children from all over the globe dress up in costumes and go around neighbors collecting food or candy while reciting the phrase "trick or treat". Some home owners further promote the idea that they are open to trick-or-treaters by putting up scary decorations and lights.

Costumes and Dress Up

On this specific holiday, children and adults dress up in a range of costumes, from supernatural figures to animated characters. Some of the most popular costumes worn worldwide include:

  • vampires
  • ghosts
  • witches
  • angels/devils
  • princesses
  • pirates
